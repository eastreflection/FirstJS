THOMAS ALVA EDISON

Thomas Edison was an american scientist.He invented many devices phonograph, the motion picture camera, and early versions of the electric light bulb. In his career he worked as a telegraph operator, which inspired some of his earliest inventions. In 1876, he established his first laboratory facility in Menlo Park, New Jersey, where many of his early inventions were developed. He had 1,093 US patents in his name, as well as patents in other countries. Edison married twice and fathered six children.

He died in 1931 of complications of diabetes.
